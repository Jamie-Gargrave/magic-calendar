National Cocoa Day

Warm up your winter on National Cocoa Day this December 13! Indulge in rich, velvety hot cocoa topped with marshmallows or explore creative recipes and delightful toppings—it's the perfect excuse to celebrate the comforting flavours of cocoa in style!

National Cocoa Day, celebrated on December 13, is a delightful occasion dedicated to enjoying the rich and comforting flavours of cocoa. This day invites everyone to indulge in a warm cup of cocoa, whether it's the classic hot cocoa made with cocoa powder or the luxurious hot chocolate crafted from ground chocolate, making it a perfect time to explore various recipes and toppings.

History of National Cocoa Day

Cocoa has a fascinating history that dates back over 5,000 years. Originally enjoyed as a bitter beverage by ancient Mesoamerican cultures, it was flavoured with vanilla and chilli rather than sugar. European explorers discovered cocoa in South America and initially found its taste unappealing, but it later became a luxury drink sweetened for the wealthy. The invention of powdered chocolate in 1828 paved the way for the chocolate bar and instant hot cocoa we know today.

How to Celebrate National Cocoa Day
  • Drink Cocoa: No celebration is complete without a steaming cup of cocoa! Make your favourite version and perhaps organise a recipe swap with friends during your tea break.
  • Try Hot Cocoa Bombs: For those feeling adventurous, why not make hot cocoa bombs? These chocolate spheres filled with cocoa and marshmallows create a fun and unique way to enjoy your drink.
  • Experiment with Toppings: Elevate your hot cocoa by trying different toppings like marshmallows, candy canes, or caramel. For a twist, consider adding spices such as cinnamon or nutmeg for extra flavour.
Why We Love National Cocoa Day

Cocoa is beloved worldwide, with countless traditional recipes enjoyed across cultures. National Cocoa Day encourages experimentation with flavours and toppings, allowing everyone to get creative with their drinks. Plus, with its rich history—from sacred rituals to modern-day treats—cocoa has come a long way, making this day perfect for sharing trivia and celebrating its delightful journey!
