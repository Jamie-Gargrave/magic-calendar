Bhutan National Day

Join the vibrant celebrations of Bhutan National Day on 17 December, a spectacular occasion that commemorates the coronation of the first king, Ugyen Wangchuck, and the rich heritage of this enchanting nation. Discover the fascinating history, traditions, and festivities that make this holiday a cherished event for Bhutanese people and visitors alike!

Bhutan National Day is a vibrant occasion that commemorates the coronation of the first king of Bhutan, Ugyen Wangchuck, in 1907. This significant holiday transforms the Changlimithang Stadium into a hub of festivities, where Bhutanese people gather to honour their rich history and the establishment of the monarchy that continues to shape their nation today.

History of Bhutan National Day

The roots of Bhutan National Day trace back to the early 17th century when the country was unified under the guidance of Buddhist monk Ngawang Namgyal. However, it was Ugyen Wangchuck, the Ponlop of Tongsa, who emerged as a pivotal figure in the late 19th century, uniting Bhutan and establishing a monarchy after abolishing the previous dual system of governance. His coronation on 17 December 1907 marked the beginning of the Wangchuck dynasty, which still reigns today as a symbol of stability and unity for the Bhutanese people.

How to Observe Bhutan National Day
  • Shut Down Your Office: Join in on this nationwide celebration by taking a break from work and immersing yourself in the festivities.
  • Prepare to Welcome Guests: The day is filled with preparations across various venues, including the Palace of the Druk and Changlimithang Ground, where everyone comes together to celebrate.
  • Visit Changlimithang Ground: Experience the excitement firsthand by attending the celebrations at this iconic venue, where dignitaries and locals gather for a day of joy.
Fascinating Facts About Bhutan National Day
  1. Commemorates a Historic Coronation: The day serves as a reminder of Ugyen Wangchuck's bold steps towards unifying Bhutan.
  2. Celebrated Annually Since 1907: This national holiday has been observed for over a century, marking 114 celebrations to date.
  3. International Attendance: Delegates from around the world come to join in on this momentous occasion.
  4. Changlimithang Ground's Legacy: This stadium has been a central site for National Day celebrations since its construction in 1974.
  5. Processions Featuring Ugyen Wangchuck's Statue: His statue is carried during processions, symbolising his lasting legacy as Bhutan's first Druk Gyalpo.
