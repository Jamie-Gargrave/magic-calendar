Monkey Day

Monkey Day is a playful celebration held annually on December 14th, dedicated to raising awareness about the conservation of primates and their habitats. Originating from a light-hearted prank by art student Casey Sorrow in 2000, it has grown into a global event that encourages education and appreciation for these remarkable animals.

Monkey Day, celebrated annually, is a delightful occasion dedicated to raising awareness about our fascinating primate friends and their conservation. Founded in 2000 by Casey Sorrow, a cheeky art student who originally wrote "Monkey Day" on a friend's calendar as a playful prank, the day has since grown into a global celebration of non-human primates.

How to Celebrate Monkey Day
  • Visit the Zoo: Many zoos around the world host special Monkey Day events, offering educational talks and unique activities that highlight primate conservation.
  • Attend Special Events: From art competitions to storytelling sessions, various institutions organise engaging programmes to educate people about primates.
  • Support Primate Charities: Consider donating to organisations dedicated to protecting endangered monkey species and their habitats.
Fun Ways to Enjoy the Day
  • Monkey-Themed Party: Host a gathering where friends dress as their favourite primates and enjoy monkey-related films and music.
  • Watch Monkey Films: Enjoy classics like "Planet of the Apes" or "Curious George" to celebrate these incredible creatures.
  • Learn and Spread Awareness: Take time to understand the challenges facing different primate species and share your knowledge with others.
Why Monkey Day Matters

Monkey Day isn't just about having a bit of fun; it's a crucial opportunity to highlight the importance of primate conservation, animal rights, and the incredible intelligence of these remarkable animals. By celebrating, we can help raise awareness about the challenges facing monkeys and other primates worldwide.
